Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

Causes

  • **Hormonal Imbalance** ‚Äì High levels of androgens (male hormones) affect ovulation.
  • **Insulin Resistance** ‚Äì Insulin helps regulate blood sugar, but resistance leads to higher insulin levels, increasing androgen production.
  • **Genetics** ‚Äì Family history can increase the risk of PCOS.
  • **Lifestyle Factors** ‚Äì Poor diet, lack of exercise, stress, and obesity contribute to worsening symptoms.

Symptoms

  • **Irregular Periods** ‚Äì Missed or infrequent periods due to ovulation issues.
  • **Weight Gain & Difficulty Losing Weight** ‚Äì Metabolic changes make weight management challenging.
  • **Excess Hair Growth (Hirsutism)** ‚Äì Unwanted hair on the face, chest, or back due to high androgen levels.
  • **Acne & Oily Skin** ‚Äì Increased testosterone levels can lead to breakouts.
  • **Hair Thinning or Hair Loss** ‚Äì Pattern baldness similar to male-pattern hair loss.
  • **Dark Skin Patches (Acanthosis Nigricans)** ‚Äì Darkened skin, especially in the neck, groin, or armpits.
  • **Mood Swings & Depression** ‚Äì PCOS can lead to emotional distress.
  • **Sleep Issues** ‚Äì Poor sleep quality or sleep apnea is common.

Diagnosis

  • Medical history and symptom assessment.
  • Physical exam to check for excess hair growth, acne, or weight gain.
  • Blood tests to measure hormone levels.
  • Ultrasound to check for ovarian cysts.

Treatment & Management

  • **Lifestyle Changes** ‚Äì Balanced diet and exercise help improve insulin sensitivity.
  • **Medications** ‚Äì Birth control pills regulate periods and reduce excess hair growth.
  • **Metformin** ‚Äì A diabetes medication that improves insulin resistance.
  • **Anti-Androgen Medications** ‚Äì Reduce symptoms like excess hair growth.
  • **Fertility Treatments** ‚Äì Ovulation medications help women trying to conceive.

Diet & Nutrition Tips

  • Eat **low glycemic index (GI) foods** ‚Äì Whole grains, nuts, legumes, and non-starchy vegetables.
  • Increase **fiber intake** ‚Äì Helps slow digestion and control blood sugar.
  • Avoid **processed foods and sugar** ‚Äì Reduce inflammation and insulin spikes.
  • Consume **healthy fats** ‚Äì Avocados, olive oil, nuts, and seeds.
  • Stay **hydrated** and reduce caffeine intake.

Complications of PCOS

  • Infertility due to irregular ovulation.
  • Increased risk of type 2 diabetes and heart disease.
  • High blood pressure and cholesterol.
  • Endometrial cancer due to irregular periods.
  • Sleep apnea and breathing issues.

PCOS and Mental Health

Many women with PCOS experience anxiety and depression due to hormonal imbalances and body image concerns. Seeking support from a therapist, joining PCOS support groups, and practicing stress management techniques can be beneficial.
